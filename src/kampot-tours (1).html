Getting Around

Transport

From Phnom Penh

Bus · 3–4 hours · $6–12

Multiple daily departures from Phnom Penh's main bus stations. Book directly with operators at the station or via WhatsApp — avoid the hotel desk markup.

  • Departures from 7:30am through to 1pm daily
  • Giant Ibis and Mekong Express are most reliable
  • Drop-off at Kampot bus station, 10 min from town
  • Tuk-tuk from station: $2–3 to riverside
  • Pre-book during peak season (Nov–Mar)

Shared Taxis

Taxi · 2.5–3 hours · $10–15

Faster than the bus and more flexible. Shared taxis run when full — typically 4 passengers. Depart from the taxi stand near Phnom Penh's central market.

  • Depart when full — usually within 30–60 min
  • Private charter available for $40–55
  • Trunk space limited — pack light or arrange delivery
  • Driver will drop at your guesthouse

Vietnam Border

Ha Tien crossing · 1.5 hours · $8–15

The Ha Tien border crossing connects Vietnam's Mekong Delta with Kampot. Direct minivans run from Ha Tien town — no need to go through Phnom Penh.

  • Ha Tien to Kampot: 1.5 hrs, mostly good road
  • Border opens 7am–5pm — do not arrive late
  • Cambodia e-visa required — apply before travel
  • Minivan drops at Kampot riverside on request

Around Town

Tuk-tuk · Moto · Rental

Kampot is small. Most of the riverside and old town is walkable. For Bokor Mountain, Kep, or the pepper farms, you'll need wheels.

  • Tuk-tuk around town: $2–4 per trip
  • Moto taxi (remork): $1–2 short distances
  • Bokor Mountain day trip by tuk-tuk: $15–20
  • Kep by tuk-tuk: $15 one way
  • Best option: rent a motorbike (see below)
